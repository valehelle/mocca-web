Three things you couldn't do before

Not filters, not keyword search — the model reads each email's content. All on your machine.

An inbox of only what matters

The AI reads each email and decides what's actually for you — personal mail, orders, bank alerts — and keeps promotions, newsletters and notifications out. It judges the content, not a list of sender rules you have to maintain.

Tell it what to do, in plain English

Write a rule in your own words — the AI reads every email and does it: label, sort, trash, unsubscribe. No filters, no regex, no conditions to wire up.

“Promotional emails — move to trash and unsubscribe.”

Ask your mailbox a question

“What did I buy from Amazon last month?” It reads the actual emails and answers — by what they say, not just subject lines or keyword matches.

Set up in three steps

Connect your email, and Mocca sorts the rest — locally.

1

Connect your email

Sign in with an app password over IMAP. Guided setup for Gmail, iCloud, Yahoo and Fastmail.

2

It sorts your mail

The on-device AI reads your latest mail, separates the important from the noise, and applies your labels and rules.

3

You stay in control

Write rules in plain words, let Mocca auto-handle the junk, and ask questions across your whole inbox.

Honest about limits: Mac only for now (Windows planned). The local model is small and not as capable as hosted models like GPT-4 — it's good at sorting and short tasks, not deep reasoning. You need a connection to fetch and send mail; reading cached mail and the on-device AI work offline. Outlook isn't supported yet (Microsoft requires OAuth, which Mocca doesn't do). It connects directly to your provider over IMAP — there are no Mocca servers.
